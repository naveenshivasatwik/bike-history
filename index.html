History of Bikes

Early Bikes

Draisine

Year: 1817

Invented by Karl von Drais, the Draisine is considered the first two-wheeled, steerable, human-propelled machine.

Penny-farthing

Year: 1870s

Known for its large front wheel and smaller rear wheel, the Penny-farthing was popular in the late 19th century.

Modern Bikes

Safety Bicycle

Year: 1880s

Featuring equal-sized wheels and a chain-driven rear wheel, the Safety Bicycle is the prototype for modern bicycles.

Mountain Bike

Year: 1970s

Designed for off-road cycling, mountain bikes have sturdy frames and wide, knobby tires for traction on rough terrain.

Electric Bike

Year: 1990s

Electric bikes, or e-bikes, feature an integrated electric motor to assist with pedaling, making cycling easier and more accessible.
